# CapX 2026 Route: Evidence

## Evidence boundary

The article is a route thesis, not a report of a completed CAP system. Architectural comparisons, benchmark proposals and supervision ladders should be read as hypotheses to validate.

## Supporting observations

- Coding agents expose planning, decomposition, tool use, inspection and iterative correction in an executable medium.
- Short closed-loop actions allow a system to observe and stop between local execution steps.
- Explicit policy code makes goals, constraints and recovery branches available for pre-execution review.

## Claims still requiring empirical validation

- Whether code-mediated policies outperform direct action policies on matched tasks and data.
- The latency and reliability envelope where longer deliberation remains useful.
- Transfer across action interfaces, robot bodies and visual conditions.
- Whether simulation-based policy audits predict real-world safety or task success.
